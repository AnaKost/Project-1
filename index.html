The Moon

The moon is the Earth's only natural satellite, and was formed nearly 4.6 billion years ago. The dark side of the moon is a myth, the moon recieves the same amount of sunlight on both sides. The moon's gravitational pull causes high and low tides in our ocean. The moon is moving approximately 3.8 cm away from our planet every year, it is estimated that this will continue to do so for another 50 billion years. The moon has no atmosphere which means that the moons surface is unprotected from cosmic rays.
