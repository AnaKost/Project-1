Callisto

Callisto is one of Jupiter's moons and is half-rocky and half-ice. Callisto has a thin atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide. Callisto is a layered moon with a rigid outer surface and an interior ocean likely made of water mixed with salts or ammonia. No activity has been detected inside Callisto and most of its features seem to be crated. Callisto has the most heavily crated surface of any world.

More Facts About Callisto

The most prominent feature of Callisto is its multi-ringed impact basin called Valhalla. It stretches across more than 1,800 kilometers and was created during a huge impact event around 2-4 billion years ago. Callisto has been explored by the Pioneer, Voyager, Galileo, Cassini, and New Horizons missions. They all studied Callisto's surface, looked for atmoshperes, or magnetic feild changes.
